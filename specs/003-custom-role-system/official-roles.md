## How to Read Character Cards

### Icons & Symbols
- **‚≠ê (Star)**: Blue Team character
- **üí£ (Bomb)**: Red Team character
- **+**: Pros/Benefits of playing this character
- **-**: Cons/Challenges of playing this character

### Card Types
- **Private Reveal Power**: Can privately show card to players
- **Public Reveal Power**: Must publicly show card to all players
- **Card Share Power**: Ability triggers when card sharing
- **Color Share Power**: Ability triggers when color sharing
- **Condition**: Starts with or gives special conditions
- **Acting**: Requires physical performance
- **Bury**: Related to buried card mechanic
- **Pause Game**: Stops game at specific time

**Description:**

**Pros:**
- Grey characters that need to know you are your natural allies
- You are a big deal and your entire team is counting on you to live

**Cons:**

## Special Power Characters

### Agent
**Type:** Private Reveal Power, Verbalization

**Power - AGENT:**
Once per round, you may privately reveal your card to a player and force that player to card share with you. You must verbally say to the target player, "I'm using my AGENT power. You must card share with me."

**Note:** The AGENT power works even on characters that normally can't card share (e.g. Shy Guy and Coy Boy).

**Pros:**
- Works well with Hunter, Medic, Zombie, and other cards with share powers

**Cons:**
- 10 players or fewer not recommended
- Contains character exceptions
- Doesn't work well with 10 players or fewer

**Recommended With:** Characters that have card share powers or restrictions

---

### Bouncer
**Type:** Private Reveal Power, Odd Player Count, Verbalization

**Power - BOUNCER:**
If you are in a room that has more players than the other room, you may privately reveal your card to any player and verbally tell them, "Get out!" When you do, that player must immediately change rooms. The BOUNCER power does not work during the last round or between rounds.

**Pros:**
- Great when there are an odd number of players

**Cons:**
- Can lead to bouncing players back and forth until the round time expires

---

### Conman
**Type:** Color Share Power

**Power - CONMAN:**
When a player agrees to color share with you, private reveal instead. They must private reveal their card too.

**Pros:**
- Players will be less willing to color share

**Cons:**
- Useless with 10 or fewer players

---

### Cupid
**Type:** Private Reveal Power, Verbalization
**Recommended Pair:** Eris

**Power - CUPID:**
Once per game, you may privately reveal your card to 2 players. You must verbally tell your target players, "You are in love with each other." Those 2 players gain the "in love" condition. Players with the "in love" condition replace their original win objective with the following win objective: Be in the same room with the player with whom you are "in love" at the end of the game or fail to win. The CUPID power cannot be used on yourself.

**Note:** If a player with the "in love" condition were to acquire the "in hate" condition, the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving that specific player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Win the game for the Red Team by using your power on the President and Bomber

**Cons:**
- It can be difficult to corral 2 players together in order to use your power

---

### Dealer
**Type:** Card Share Power

**Power - DEALER:**
Any player that card shares with you gains the "foolish" condition. Players with the "foolish" condition can never turn down an offer to card or color share.

**Note:** If a player with the "foolish" condition were to acquire a contradictory condition (e.g. "shy" or "coy"), the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving the player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Criminal recommended

**Cons:**
- Information can get very public very quickly

---

### Doctor
**Type:** Card Share Power
**Recommended Pair:** Engineer

**Special Team Win Condition:**
When playing with the Doctor, the Blue Team has the following additional win condition: the President must card share with the Doctor before the end of the game or the Blue Team loses. At the end of the game, the President will be asked if he/she card shared with the Doctor. At that time both players will verify or deny having card shared.

**Note:** Remember that if the President or Bomber character ever switches player control (perhaps due to the Hot Potato), then the new President/Bomber must card share with the Doctor/Engineer to prevent losing.

**Pros:**
- Characters with card share powers recommended with Engineer and Doctor

**Cons:**
- Games with the Doctor and Engineer may lead to a game where all players lose

---

### Dr. Boom
**Type:** Card Share Power
**Recommended Pair:** Tuesday Knight

**Power - BOOM:**
If you card share with the President, everyone in the same room as you instantly gains the "dead" condition and the game ends.

**Note:** The BOOM power never works on the President's Daughter. If the President is buried, the BOOM power is never used.

**Pros:**
- Characters with card share powers recommended

**Cons:**
- Games can last less than 30 seconds
- Without Tuesday Knight, Red Team has an advantage
- Doesn't work well with characters that pause the game

---

### Enforcer
**Type:** Private Reveal Power, Verbalization

**Power - ENFORCER:**
Once per round, you may privately reveal your card to 2 players. You must verbally tell your target players, "You must reveal your cards to one another." Those 2 players must card share with one another (not to you). You cannot use this power on yourself, but another Enforcer can use their power on you.

**Note:** The ENFORCER power works even on characters that normally can't card share (e.g. Shy Guy).

**Pros:**
- Recommended with 11 or more players

**Cons:**
- Doesn't work well with 10 players or fewer

Players with the "in hate" condition replace their original win objective with the following win objective: be in the opposite room of the player with whom you are "in hate" at the end of the game or fail to win. **Note:** If a player with the "in hate" condition were to acquire the "in love" condition, the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving that specific player with neither condition.

### Mayor
**Type:** Public Reveal Power

**Power - MAYOR:**
If your room has an even number of players, you may publicly reveal your card when attempting to usurp a leader. Your vote to usurp counts as 2 votes instead of 1 unless the opposing Mayor also publicly reveals their card.

**Pros:**
- Rooms that have an even number of players recommended

**Cons:**
- Pointless in games with 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, 26, or 30 players

---

### Medic
**Type:** Card Share Power

**Power - MEDIC:**
Any player that card shares with you has all "conditions" removed. This does not make yourself immune to acquiring conditions. The opposing Medic can remove your acquired conditions.

**Pros:**
- Characters that provide and start with conditions recommended

**Cons:**
- Zombie not recommended

---

### Psychologist
**Type:** Card Share Power, Private Reveal Power

**Power - PSYCHOLOGIST:**
When you privately reveal your card to a character with a psych condition (e.g. "shy", "coy", etc.), that character may then immediately card share with you. If they do, their psych condition is removed.

**Pros:**
- Characters that provide or begin with psych conditions recommended

**Cons:**
- Without psych conditions, this character is like man nipples... nearly pointless

---

### Security
**Type:** Public Reveal Power, Verbalization

**Power - TACKLE:**
Publicly reveal your card, immediately pick any player in the room (besides yourself), then verbally tell them, "You're going nowhere." However, your card must permanently remain publicly revealed for the rest of the game. This means that you can only use this power once. The target of your TACKLE power can't leave as a hostage this round.

**Pros:**
- Players will not likely need to guess the other room's hostage

**Cons:**
- Unfortunately, this character is slightly more powerful for the Blue Team

---

### Usurper
**Type:** Public Reveal Power

**Power - USURPER:**
During any round but the last, you may publicly reveal your card and become the leader. However, your card must permanently remain publicly revealed for the rest of the game. This means that you can only use this power once. You cannot be usurped during the same round in which you used your USURPER power, not even by another Usurper. If 2 Usurpers use their power in the same room during the same round, whichever Usurper used their power first remains the leader, the other Usurper wasted their power.

**Pros:**
- Mastermind and the Minion recommended

**Cons:**
- After using USURPER power, traditional card sharing and revealing can't be done

## Condition-Based Characters

### Angel
**Type:** Acting, Condition
**Recommended Pair:** Demon

**Condition - HONEST:**
You begin with the "honest" condition. Players with the "honest" condition must always verbally tell the truth. This means that you are permitted to lie as long as it is not verbally.

**Note:** If a player with the "honest" condition were to acquire the "liar" condition, the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving the player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Demon recommended

**Cons:**
- Pathological liars may have a difficult time as this character

---

### Blind
**Type:** Acting, Condition

**Condition - BLIND:**
You begin with the "blind" condition. Players with the "blind" condition must do their best to never open their eyes during the game. Don't worry, it is a short game.

**Pros:**
- Players that aren't maniacal douchebags recommended

**Cons:**
- Tuesday Knight Games is not responsible for injuries during play
- Mummy not recommended

---

### Coy Boy
**Type:** Psych Condition
**Recommended With:** Spy and Negotiator

**Condition - COY:**
You begin with the "coy" condition. Players with the "coy" condition may ONLY color share unless a character's power forces otherwise.

**Note:** The "coy" condition is a psych condition and can be cured by a Psychologist.

**Another Note:** If a player with the "coy" condition were to acquire the "foolish" condition, the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving the player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Spy and Negotiator recommended

**Cons:**
- Pointless with 10 players or fewer as this essentially makes the Coy Boy "shy"

---

### Criminal
**Type:** Card Share Power
**Recommended With:** Psychologist, Engineer, and Doctor

**Power - CRIMINAL:**
Any player that card shares with you gains the "shy" condition. Players with the "shy" condition may not reveal any part of their card to any player.

**Note:** If a player with the "foolish" condition were to acquire the "shy" condition, the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving the player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Psychologist, Engineer, and Doctor recommended

**Cons:**
- Private Eye, Mastermind, or Zombie are not recommended

- Characters with card share powers recommended

### Zombie
**Type:** Card Share Power, Color Share Power, Condition, Contagious
**Recommended With:** Doctor, Engineer, and Privacy Promise rule variant

**Pros:**

**Cons:**

## Card Swap Characters

### Drunk
**Type:** Card Swap

**Setup:**
Before characters cards are dealt but after they are shuffled, randomly remove a character card. This is the "sober" character card. Place the "sober" card facedown in a location easily accessible to all players (usually between the 2 rooms). Then shuffle the Drunk card into the remaining deck of character cards.

**Power:**
At the beginning of the last round of the game, the Drunk character should trade their Drunk card with the "sober" card. Assume all powers and responsibilities associated with the "sober" character card.

**Lose Condition:**
You lose if you forget or are unable to trade your card for the "sober" card.

**Note:** The "sober" card is always cleansed when it is first retrieved, meaning it has no acquired conditions.

**Another Note:** If you don't retrieve the "sober" card at the beginning of the last round, you're still considered to be the "sober" character.

**Pros:**
- This is a crowd favorite card

**Cons:**
- Subtly trading the Drunk card for the "sober" card is difficult

---

### Hot Potato
**Type:** Card Share Power, Card Swap, Color Share Power
**Recommended With:** Privacy Promise rule variant and more players

**Power - HOT POTATO:**
Any player that card shares or color shares with you immediately trades cards with you. Both you and the other player assume the powers and the allegiance of the newly acquired cards. The Hot Potato loses at the end of the game.

**Note:** Due to the cleanse rule, any previously acquired conditions (e.g. "cursed") are lost when receiving a new character card.

**Pros:**
- Recommended with the Privacy Promise rule variant and more players

**Cons:**
- Doesn't work well with characters that have the "foolish" condition

---

### Leprechaun
**Type:** Card Share Power, Card Swap, Color Share Power, Condition
**Recommended With:** More players and Privacy Promise rule variant

**Condition - FOOLISH:**
You begin with the "foolish" condition. Players with the "foolish" condition can never turn down an offer to card or color share.

**Power - LEPRECHAUN:**
Any player that card shares or even color shares with you immediately trades cards with you. Both you and the other player assume the powers and the allegiance of the newly acquired cards. At the end of the game, the Leprechaun wins. A single player can only ever be the Leprechaun once per game. If a player is about to become the Leprechaun character for the second time, they must communicate that they can't receive the Leprechaun card.

**Note:** Due to the cleanse rule, any previously acquired conditions (e.g. "cursed") are lost when receiving a new character card.

**Another Note:** If a player with the "foolish" condition were to acquire a contradictory condition (e.g. "shy" or "coy"), the 2 conditions would cancel one another, leaving the player with neither condition.

**Pros:**
- Works well with more players and with the Privacy Promise rule variant

**Cons:**
- Can cause a whole lot of confusion

**Power - MUMMY:**
Any player that card shares with you gains the "cursed" condition. Players with the "cursed" condition must do their best to not make any noise.

**Note:** Because of the MUMMY power, "cursed" players are prevented from using any powers or abilities that requires a verbalization (e.g. Agent, Enforcer, etc.).

**Pros:**
- Mime and Medic recommended
- Best when played with folks who love charades

**Cons:**
- Blind not recommended
- Forces players to play charades

---

### Paparazzo
**Type:** Acting, Public Reveal Power
**Recommended With:** Privacy Promise variant

**Acting Requirement:**
Do your best to make sure there are no private conversations. Be as intrusive and nosy as possible without actually physically manipulating others. If playing with the Privacy Promise rule variant, ignore the rule as long as you publicly reveal your card to prove to others that you are permitted to be invasive.

**Pros:**
- Privacy Promise variant recommended
- Gives you an excuse to be annoying

**Cons:**
- You're annoying

## Game-Pausing Characters

### Anarchist
**Recommended Pair:** Minion

**Win Condition:**
You win if your vote helped successfully usurp a leader during a majority of the rounds. For example, in a 3 round game, you must have usurped a leader 2 of the 3 rounds.

**Pros:**
- Minion recommended

**Cons:**
- Not recommended for those that can't start a movement
- The Minion is your natural enemy

---

### Gambler
**Type:** Pause Game 10

**Win Condition:**
At the end of the last round, before all players reveal their cards, you must publicly announce which team (Red Team, Blue Team, or neither) you think won the game. Win only if you are correct.

**Note:** You have a pause game number of 10. Only pause the game and make your announcement after all characters with a lower pause game number (e.g. Private Eye) have finished their announcements.

**Pros:**
- A great character for the basic game to even out teams if there are an odd number of players

**Cons:**
- Sometimes players forget that the game is suppose to pause before everyone reveals

---

### Minion

**Win Condition:**
You win if a leader is never usurped in the same room as you.

**Pros:**
- Mastermind and Usurper recommended

**Cons:**
- Can get boring if paired with a very stable leader
- Tough to win when the Usurper is in play

---

### Private Eye
**Type:** Pause Game 05
**Recommended:** 10 players or less

**Win Condition:**
At the end of the last round, before all players reveal their character cards, you must publicly announce the identity of the buried card. Win only if you are correct.

**Note:** You have a pause game number of 5. Only pause the game and make your announcement after all characters with a lower pause game number have finished their announcements.

**Pros:**
- 10 players or less recommended

**Cons:**
- Characters that restrict information sharing not recommended (e.g. Criminal)

---

### Sniper
**Type:** Pause Game 15
**Pair:** Target, Decoy

**Win Condition:**
At the end of the last round, before all players reveal their character cards, you must publicly announce which player you are shooting. The selected player does not have to be in the same room as you. You win if the player you selected is the Target.

**Note:** You have a pause game number of 15. Only pause the game and make your announcement after all characters with a lower pause game number (e.g. Private Eye) have finished their announcements.

**Pros:**
- This is a tough character to be, but shooting the Target player feels amazing

**Cons:**
- Characters that card swap not recommended

## Special Characters

### Ambassador
**Type:** Condition
**Player Count:** 11 or more players recommended

**Special Rules:**
As soon as you are dealt your card, announce "I am an Ambassador!" Your card is permanently publicly revealed. You have the "immune" condition. Players with the "immune" condition are immune to all powers and conditions. Ambassadors can walk freely between the 2 rooms. Ambassadors are never considered a part of a room's population. Therefore, Ambassadors can never take part in any vote, be hostages, be leaders, and they can never be targeted by abilities.

**Note:** Ambassadors don't count towards the player count in the game. This means if you have 18 players including the Ambassadors, you should be playing a 16 player game. Ambassadors don't count for or against Team Zombie's win objective.

**Pros:**
- 11 or more players recommended

**Cons:**
- Do not bury Ambassadors
- There must be 2 Ambassadors for proper game balance

---

### Tuesday Knight
**Type:** Card Share Power
**Recommended Pair:** Dr. Boom

**Power - HUG:**
If you card share with the Bomber, everyone in the same room as you, except the President, gains the "dead" condition and the game instantly ends.

**Note:** The HUG power never works on the Martyr. If the Bomber is buried, the HUG power is never used.

**Pros:**
- Characters with card share powers recommended

**Cons:**
- Games can last less than 30 seconds
- Without Dr. Boom, Blue Team has the advantage
- Doesn't work well with characters that pause the game (e.g. Sniper, Private Eye, etc.)
